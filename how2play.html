Cards Cricket

Objective

The aim is to score as many runs as possible in 20 overs while avoiding getting all out.

Setup

  • Deck of Cards: Use four standard 52-card decks.
  • Score sheets: Download from the Internet or draw one to add the team.
  • Players: Two or more players. One acts as the batsman and the other as the bowler.

Rules

Card Values:

  • Number cards (2-10): Represent runs (0,1,2,3,4 and 6 runs respectively).
  • Face cards (Joker, Jack, Queen, King, Ace): Removed from the deck.
  • Batsman is considered out:
    • During the run of the game, the bowler checkmates two playing cards played by the batsman with similar color and number cards.
    • The batsman is considered bowled out if three consecutive cards played by the batsman are checkmated by the bowler of any color card.

Gameplay:

  • Combine and shuffle the four decks to arrive at 144 playing cards.
  • The bowler (dealer) alternately deals one card at a time between the bowler and the batsman until 6 cards are dealt to the batsman, and the bowler receives 9 cards (additional 3 cards) to complete the first over.
  • The batsman accumulates runs based on the difference in numbers on the cards played ball by ball, recorded in a scorebook.
  • The batsman continues to bat until all 120 cards are drawn, they are out, or 20 overs are completed.

Variations

  • Overs: Introduce 10 overs per game (a set number of cards per over) for a shorter, structured game.
  • Teams: Form teams and take turns batting and bowling, similar to real cricket.
  • Scoring: Keep a tally of scores over multiple innings or games to determine the winner.

Example

Player A (bowler/dealer) draws a ♥5 from their hand and plays it on the table. Player B places a ♥8, scoring runs based on two conditions: similar color card and higher difference in numbers. The score of 3 runs is determined by the AI bot.

Winning

The player or team with the most runs at the end of the 20 overs wins.
